Brisa is a breeze, in a can. A premium sparkling water for people who want lift without weight — minerality, a whisper of flavor, and nothing that asks for attention. This volume covers everything from the droplet glyph to the words we won't put on a label.

§ 01 — Front matter

What Brisa is.

A premium sparkling water for the urban wellness market. Glass-clear liquid, mineral-forward palate, minimal sugar, no caffeine. Brisa belongs on the desk, the mat, and the dinner table — quietly.

Promise

Lift, without weight.

The way air moves through an open window. The way a citrus peel sharpens a glass of water. Brisa is the smallest possible interruption between you and your day — and the most welcome one.

Audience

25–40, urban, deliberate.

They read labels. They train. They host. They've outgrown sweetness but haven't given up on flavor. They will pay for a can that earns its place on the counter.

P · 01

Light is the discipline.

Every choice — palette, type, copy, packaging — defaults to less. If a thing can come out, it comes out. The breeze is what's left.

P · 02

Minerality, never sweetness.

Brisa speaks the language of stone, citrus peel, and morning air. We never lean on sugar metaphors, dessert words, or candy-shop colour.

P · 03

One claim per surface.

Wellness is crowded. We make one promise at a time and leave room for the consumer to bring their own. A can is not an essay.

P · 04

Brisa before Onda.

On every consumer surface, Brisa leads. The Onda parent appears only in fine print: "Brisa is a brand of Onda." See § 09.

§ 02 — Mark

The marks.

A handwritten-italic wordmark and a single rising droplet. That's it. The droplet is the first bubble in the can. The italic is the breeze the bubble travels on.

Clear space & minimum size

Air around the breeze.

Clear space equals the height of the lowercase b on all sides. Minimum size for the wordmark is 40px digital, 10mm print. The droplet glyph holds down to 16px on its own.

The flavor-accent rule

Accent on the can. Aqua on the chrome.

Each flavor owns a single accent colour, used on the can artwork and inside campaign creative. UI chrome — buttons, links, focus rings, loading states — always uses Brisa's primary aqua, regardless of which flavor the asset is selling. This is what keeps the brand legible across a 60-asset campaign.

§ 04 — Typography

Three typefaces.

Instrument Serif carries the heart — italic-leaning, editorial, airy. Geist runs every functional surface. Geist Mono carries metadata, pack copy, and SKU. Three faces, no exceptions.

Geist is the workhorse. Clean geometry, generous x-height, high legibility on small screens. It carries every piece of functional copy on a Brisa surface — ingredients, product descriptions, store locator, e-commerce. It is intentionally quiet so the italic display always wins the room.

Inheritance from Onda

Brisa does not inherit Onda's palette.

Onda is oceanic deep blue; Brisa is mineral teal. The two parent and child intentionally do not share accent. Sub-brand colour belongs to the sub-brand alone — see Onda § 08, the co-branding rule.

Light & atmosphere

The breeze is fixed.

Every Brisa page carries a fixed atmosphere wash — three soft radial gradients (foam, aqua, sun) layered behind the content. It is the brand's signature ambient layer. Never disable it on a Brisa surface; never enable it on an Onda or Volt one.

What the pipeline checks before publish

Three gates. One green light.

① Mark present. Every asset carries the Brisa wordmark or droplet at minimum size. ② Palette in-bounds. Flavor accent matches the declared SKU; UI chrome stays aqua. ③ Banned words clear. Headline runs the § 08 banned list before rendering. Any flag blocks publish and routes to a human reviewer.

§ 08 — Voice

How Brisa speaks.

Brisa's voice is the breeze: short, warm, declarative. It uses sensory language — peel, stone, morning, lift — and avoids wellness jargon, sugar metaphors, and category clichés.

We are

Bright, mineral, declarative. A short sentence with a long after-taste.

We are not

Loud, slangy, ironic, or aspirational about hustle. Volt does loud. Brisa doesn't.

We say

"Lemon peel, blood orange, a flick of yuzu. Open one cold."

We don't say

"Hydration goals 💦 — guilt-free, sugar-free, calorie-free, judgement-free."

Banned words

Words and phrases the pipeline flags before any Brisa asset ships. Some are legal (health claims). Some are voice (off-tone). All are non-negotiable.

§ 09 — Parent

How Brisa answers to Onda.

Brisa is sub-brand 001 in the Onda registry. The relationship is governed by Onda § 08 — co-branding & governance. These are the four rules Brisa carries from that document.

Rule 01 · The fine print

"A brand of Onda."

Every Brisa packaging back panel, legal footer, and corporate page carries the line "Brisa is a brand of Onda Beverages" — set in Geist Mono, 11px, mineral or foam depending on context. That is the only place Onda appears on a Brisa surface.

Rule 02 · No co-lockup

Never side by side.

The Brisa wordmark and the Onda wordmark do not appear together on a hero asset, a can, or a paid post. Lockups are reserved for corporate, regulatory, and HR contexts only.

Rule 03 · Palette stays home

Aqua is Brisa's.

Brisa never borrows Onda's tide blue. Onda never borrows Brisa's aqua. Each brand's palette stops at its own boundary.

Rule 04 · Voice stays home

Brisa never sounds like Volt.

A Brisa asset that uses Volt's punchy energy-drink tone is a governance failure, even if the words are technically on-brand for Volt. The pipeline catches this before publish.
