Volt is performance you can hear. A premium energy drink for the gym floor, the late shift, and the last lap. This volume covers everything from the bolt glyph to the words a human reviewer kicks back before the can ships.

§ 01 — Front matter

What Volt is.

A premium energy drink for the 18–28 active lifestyle market. 200mg caffeine, taurine, B-complex, zero sugar. Volt is the opposite of Brisa by design — loud, charged, and unapologetic.

Promise

Performance you can hear.

The hum before the lift. The crack of the tab in a quiet room. The first sip that arrives a half-second before you do. Volt doesn't whisper — and the people who drink it don't either.

Audience

18–28. Active. On the move.

Lifters, gamers, riders, runners, late-shift workers. They train in front of mirrors and post the set. They want a drink that shows up the way they do — not the wellness aisle.

P · 01

Loud is the discipline.

Volt's loudness is structural, not decorative. It shows up as 700-weight type, hard edges, full-bleed Volt Yellow, and zero-radius corners. If a surface looks soft, it's wrong.

P · 02

Charge, not aggression.

Volt is energy, not anger. We never reach for macho clichés, gym-bro put-downs, or hustle-porn. The brand is confident enough not to need them. See § 08.

P · 03

One unit, one watt.

Every Volt asset states one benefit. Caffeine, focus, endurance, recovery — pick one per surface and own it. Stacking claims is how a brand starts sounding like a label.

P · 04

Volt before Onda.

On every consumer surface, Volt leads. The Onda parent appears only in fine print: "Volt is a brand of Onda." See § 09.

§ 02 — Mark

The marks.

A capitalized slab wordmark with a bolt-cut "O", and a freestanding bolt glyph. The wordmark is the brand at full volume; the bolt is the brand at glance distance — on a phone, a hat, a sleeve.

Clear space & minimum size

Don't crowd the current.

Clear space equals the height of the L in VOLT on all sides. Minimum size for the wordmark is 56px digital, 14mm print. The bolt glyph holds down to 20px on its own. The bolt is never set in any colour other than Volt Yellow or pure black.

The flavor-accent rule

Accent on the can. Volt on the chrome.

Each flavor owns a single accent — Ion blue for Original, Plasma orange for Tropic. Accents appear only on cans and inside campaign creative. UI chrome — buttons, focus rings, links, status — always uses Volt Yellow, regardless of which SKU the asset is selling. This is what keeps the brand legible across a 60-asset campaign.

Inheritance from Onda

Volt does not inherit Onda's palette.

Onda is oceanic deep blue; Volt is carbon-and-current. The two parent and child intentionally do not share accent. Volt borrows the registry's Amber Soft (#FFD93D) only for the parent-context card on the Onda corporate site — never on a Volt surface. See Onda § 08.

§ 06 — Foundation

Foundation.

Volt keeps Onda's 4-px spacing scale. It departs sharply on radii (zero by default), shadows (hard offsets, no blur), and motion (faster easing, no float). Volt does not bounce, drift, or fade.

No pill. No 999. If you reach for a pill button, you're working on the wrong brand.

Motion

Snap, never float.

Default ease: cubic-bezier(0.2, 0, 0, 1). Default duration: 120ms for state, 200ms for surfaces. Hover effects on Volt translate the element 2px on each axis with a hard offset shadow — never a soft drop. All transitions collapse under prefers-reduced-motion: reduce.

Charge & atmosphere

Strobe + scanline.

Every Volt page carries two fixed atmosphere layers: a soft volt/amber radial glow, and a 4-pixel scanline overlay. Together they're the brand's signature charge texture. Never disable on a Volt surface; never enable on an Onda or Brisa one.

What the pipeline checks before publish

Three gates. One green light.

① Mark present. Every asset carries the Volt wordmark or bolt at minimum size. ② Palette in-bounds. Flavor accent matches the declared SKU; UI chrome stays Volt Yellow. ③ Banned words clear. Headline runs the § 08 banned list before rendering. Any flag blocks publish and routes to a human reviewer — not a model retry.

§ 08 — Voice

How Volt speaks.

Volt's voice is the current: short, charged, confident. Sensory verbs — pull, crack, lift, run, shift, finish. Numbers and nouns over adjectives. Volt is the loudest brand in the room without ever raising its voice into aggression.

We are

Punchy, direct, charged. Verbs first. Numbers second. Adjectives last.

We are not

Wellness-soft, gym-bro, hustle-porn, or ironic. Brisa does soft. Volt doesn't.

We say

"200mg. Zero sugar. Pull the tab."

We don't say

"Crush every workout. Unleash your beast mode. No excuses, only gains. 💪"

Banned words

Words and phrases the pipeline flags before any Volt asset ships. Some are legal (function claims). Some are voice (off-tone). All are non-negotiable.

§ 09 — Parent

How Volt answers to Onda.

Volt is sub-brand 002 in the Onda registry. The relationship is governed by Onda § 08 — co-branding & governance. These are the four rules Volt carries from that document.

Rule 01 · The fine print

"A brand of Onda."

Every Volt packaging back panel, legal footer, and corporate page carries the line "Volt is a brand of Onda Beverages" — set in JetBrains Mono, 11px, paper or carbon depending on context. That is the only place Onda appears on a Volt surface.

Rule 02 · No co-lockup

Never side by side.

The Volt wordmark and the Onda wordmark do not appear together on a hero asset, a can, or a paid post. Lockups are reserved for corporate, regulatory, and HR contexts only.

Rule 03 · Palette stays home

Volt is Volt's.

Volt never borrows Onda's tide blue. Onda never reaches for Volt Yellow on a corporate surface. Each brand's palette stops at its own boundary.

Rule 04 · Voice stays home

Volt never sounds like Brisa.

A Volt asset that uses Brisa's quiet editorial italics is a governance failure, even if the words are technically on-brand for Brisa. The pipeline catches this before publish.
