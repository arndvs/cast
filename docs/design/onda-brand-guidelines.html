Onda is the parent. It does not ship a bottle, can, or pouch — it shepherds the brands that do. This volume governs how Onda looks, sounds, and grants identity to every wave that follows from it.

§ 01 — Front matter

What Onda is.

A holding company in the beverage category, headquartered globally, operated locally. Onda owns the corporate identity, the standards, and the registry. Each sub-brand owns its category, its consumer, and its voice.

Mission

Make the wave. Then get out of its way.

Onda exists to give consumer brands the back-of-house they need to move fast — distribution, governance, creative ops — without ever crowding the shelf they appear on.

What Onda is not

A consumer brand.

You will not see Onda on a can. You will see it in the fine print, the boardroom, the brand portal, and the regulatory filing. Brisa pours. Volt pours. Onda holds.

P · 01

One sea, many waves.

Every Onda sub-brand is a different wave from the same body of water. They share a parent, a registry, and a standards layer — and almost nothing else.

P · 02

Sub-brands lead. Onda follows.

In market, the consumer brand is the hero. The Onda mark is reserved for parent contexts: corporate communication, governance, regulatory, and packaging fine print.

P · 03

Govern the system, not the surface.

Onda standards cover process: what each brand must declare, how it inherits tokens, how violations get flagged. We do not dictate sub-brand aesthetics.

P · 04

Velocity is a brand value.

The portfolio model exists so brands ship faster. Anything that slows a sub-brand without protecting the parent is friction — and friction is a defect.

How a sub-brand inherits

Inheritance is opt-in. Identity is opt-out.

A sub-brand inherits Onda's governance by default — the registry entry, the legal voice, the corporate footer, the portfolio reference. It opts out of Onda's identity by default — its own palette, type, mark, and consumer voice. Crossing the boundary requires a declared lockup (see § 08).

§ 03 — Marks

The marks.

The Onda parent mark is a wordmark with a single tide-blue stop. The stop is the wave's origin point — the sea before it becomes a wave. Use the mark sparingly; the parent is rarely the hero.

Clear space & minimum size

Give it room. Or don't show it at all.

Clear space equals the height of the lowercase o on all sides. Minimum size is 72px for digital, 14mm for print. Below that, omit the mark entirely — the parent does not need to whisper.

Ink, tide, sand.

Rule of thumb

If it's loud, it isn't Onda.

Saturated reds, neon yellows, and electric magentas belong to the sub-brands (and only some of them). The parent palette stays oceanic and architectural so it can host any sub-brand without collision.

§ 08 — Co-branding & governance

When Onda appears.

The parent mark only appears in four contexts. Anywhere else, the sub-brand stands alone. This is the most-violated rule in the system, and the one the registry checks for before any asset ships.

The four parent contexts

Corporate · regulatory · governance · fine print.

Annual reports, investor decks, and corporate communications — Onda leads. Regulatory disclosures and legal notices — Onda is the entity. Internal governance surfaces (the brand portal, the registry, this document) — Onda is the host. Packaging fine print — "a brand of Onda", set in mono, at the foot of the panel.

Do · Don't

✓ Do

Lead with the sub-brand on the can, the spot, the post. Reference Onda only in fine print: "Brisa is a brand of Onda."

✗ Don't

Stack the Onda mark beside the Brisa mark on a hero asset. The consumer is meeting Brisa, not its parent.

✓ Do

Inherit Onda's palette only on parent surfaces. The brand portal uses tide. Brisa packaging never does.

✗ Don't

Borrow Volt's amber for an Onda corporate slide. Sub-brand colour belongs to the sub-brand alone.

✓ Do

Run every campaign asset through the registry's brand-check before publish. Logos present, palette in-bounds, banned-word list clear.

✗ Don't

Mix sub-brand voices in a single asset. A Brisa post that uses Volt's punchy tone is a governance failure, even if the words are on-brand for Volt.

The lockup exception

Portfolio lockups exist. They are rare.

A formal Onda × Sub-brand lockup is permitted in three cases: a corporate press release announcing the brand, a regulatory filing referencing the entity, and an internal HR or finance document. It is not permitted on any consumer-facing surface without legal sign-off.

§ 09 — Voice

Parent voice.

Onda speaks in the corporate register: precise, multi-lingual, structural, unhurried. It never tries to be the sub-brand. The voice below covers every parent surface — investor letters, governance docs, the brand portal.

We are

Architectural. Multilingual. Quietly confident in the system we built.

We are not

Loud, clever, or consumer-facing. Save the volume for the brands that need it.

We say

"The portfolio shipped 142 campaigns this quarter across nine markets."

We don't say

"We crushed it this Q. ⚡ Onda is the wave you didn't know you needed."

When in doubt

Defer to the sub-brand's voice. The parent is rarely the speaker.

Inheritance, in voice

Sub-brands write their own voice charters.

See Brisa Brand Guidelines and Volt Brand Guidelines for the consumer voices used in market. Onda's only voice obligation to its sub-brands is the corporate footer line — set in mono, at the foot of every legal surface: A brand of Onda Beverages.
